Brand Context

Why Lenskart?

Lenskart has built an incredible product and retail network but their Instagram content plays it too safe. Their current feed is product-heavy without enough storytelling, lifestyle context, or save-worthy value. The brand has personality (Blue Light glasses, quirky campaigns, strong CEO presence) but it doesn't consistently show up in the content.

This 10-day plan is built on 4 content pillars: Education (eye health, lens types), Lifestyle (frames as identity/fashion), Product (showcase + conversions), and Community (UGC, engagement). Each day has a clear format, a goal, and a "why now" rationale.

The Thinking Behind It

Strategy Notes

Why these posting times?

Indian Instagram audience peaks at 12 to 2 PM (lunch scroll) and 7 to 9 PM (post-dinner). Weekends shift earlier 10 AM to 1 PM. I've aligned every post to one of these windows based on content intent (shopping vs. casual consumption).

Why this format mix?

Reels = reach. Carousels = saves. Stories = engagement. Statics = product presence. A healthy account uses all four. This plan intentionally rotates formats so the algorithm sees variety and the audience doesn't get bored.

The saves obsession

Saves are Instagram's highest-intent signal. A post someone saves is a post they plan to return to. I've built Days 2, 6, and 9 specifically to be saved cheat sheets, budget lists, identity content.

Balancing sell vs. value

Only 3 out of 10 posts are directly product/conversion-led. The other 7 build trust, provide value, or entertain. Audiences who trust you buy from you. Audiences who feel sold to unfollow you.

"Every post should have a job. If it doesn't, don't post it."
