The Setup

Why These Three?

Mamaearth, Plum, and Minimalist are three of India's most prominent D2C skincare brands all operating in the same space, targeting broadly the same audience (18 to 34, skincare-aware, digitally native), and all investing heavily in Instagram. But their approaches are radically different, and the results show it.

I analyzed their last 30 days of Instagram content across 6 dimensions: content variety, posting frequency, engagement rate, visual consistency, community interaction, and audience sentiment in comments.

The Verdict: Minimalist Wins and Here's Why

Minimalist wins this comparison decisively, and the reason is simple: they treat Instagram as an education platform, not just an advertising one. Their content makes people feel smarter about ingredients, about routines, about their own skin. That creates trust. Trust creates saves. Saves create algorithm reach. Reach creates sales. The entire funnel flows from one clear strategic decision: be genuinely useful. Mamaearth has more followers but less influence. Plum has personality but lacks the depth. Minimalist has built the kind of social presence that doesn't just sell products it builds a category.

What Brands Can Learn

Key Lessons

01

Engagement rate > follower count, every time

Mamaearth has 2.7x more followers than Minimalist but 4x lower engagement. A smaller, more engaged audience converts at a higher rate and costs less to maintain.

02

Education builds loyalty that promotion never can

When a brand teaches you something, you trust them. Minimalist's ingredient-education posts create brand equity that no amount of discount posts can buy.

03

Visual consistency is non-negotiable for recognition

A brand should be identifiable from a single post without seeing the username. Minimalist nails this. Mamaearth doesn't. At scale, this difference is massive.

04

Reply to your comments seriously

A comment reply takes 10 seconds and signals to the algorithm that this post is generating conversation. Mamaearth's silence in their comments section is a missed growth opportunity every single day.

"Winning on social is a strategy problem, not a budget problem."
