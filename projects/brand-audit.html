Why Zomato

The Brand I Chose

Zomato is one of India's most talked-about brands on social media not just in the food-tech space, but across the entire digital landscape. They've built a reputation for bold, often irreverent social content, especially on X (Twitter). But bold doesn't always mean effective. I wanted to go deeper: where is their strategy genuinely working, where are they leaving engagement on the table, and what would I do differently?

This audit covers their Instagram and X presence, looking at content pillars, posting cadence, engagement quality, visual consistency, and missed opportunities.

Content Pillars Analysis

What They Post

Zomato's content breaks down into roughly 5 pillars: meme culture & relatability, food mood content, product/feature announcements, trend-jacking, and user-generated reposts. On X, they lean heavily into witty one-liners and cultural commentary. On Instagram, it's a mix of Reels (food cravings, mood-based content) and carousels.

The X strategy is genuinely strong they've built a distinctive brand voice that feels human and in-on-the-joke. Instagram, however, tells a different story. The visual identity is inconsistent, the Reels are often riding trends too late, and the save-worthy content is sparse.

What's Working

  • Distinctly human brand voice on X feels like a person, not a brand
  • High share rate on X due to humour and cultural relevance
  • Strong top-of-funnel awareness from meme-style content
  • Fast response to trending moments (X is their real strength)
  • Community-driven content (reposts, comment replies, banter)
  • Seasonal and festival content feels native and authentic

What's Not Working

  • Instagram visual identity is inconsistent no clear grid aesthetic
  • Reels often trend-jack too late (7 to 10 days after peak)
  • Very low save rate content entertains but doesn't add value
  • No educational or how-to content to build authority
  • Product features buried in bland announcement graphics
  • Almost zero UGC strategy on Instagram
  • CTAs are weak or missing entirely on most posts
Posting Cadence & Timing

The Timing Problem

Zomato posts frequently almost daily on both platforms. On X, this works because the platform rewards real-time relevance. On Instagram, volume without strategy is noise. Their Reels are being published 7 to 10 days after a trend peaks, which means the algorithm has already stopped amplifying that audio or format. They're consistently late to their own party.

Their best-performing time windows based on audience activity (Indian audience, primarily 18 to 34) should be 12 to 2 PM (lunchtime scroll) and 8 to 10 PM (post-dinner window). From observable patterns, they're not consistently hitting these windows.

My Recommendations

What I'd Fix

01

Build a 3-column grid identity on Instagram

Alternate between: food mood visuals (warm, saturated), text-led opinion/wit posts (on-brand dark bg), and Reels thumbnails. Create a grid that tells you who you are before you even read a caption.

02

Create a trend radar system post within 48 hours

Assign someone to monitor trending audio and formats daily. A trend-jack that lands on day 2 outperforms one that lands on day 9 by a factor of 10x in reach. Speed is the strategy.

03

Introduce save-worthy content (food tips, city guides)

"Best Biryani in Mumbai under ₹200" that's a saves machine. Instagram's algorithm weights saves heavily. One carousel like this per week would compound reach significantly over 3 months.

04

Launch a UGC campaign with a branded hashtag

Create a challenge or hashtag that incentivizes customers to post their orders. Repost the best ones weekly. This builds community, generates free content, and creates social proof all at once.

05

Reframe product announcements as storytelling

Instead of: "Introducing Zomato Gold 2.0." Try: "You told us the old version was mid. We listened." Same information. Completely different engagement. The brand voice on X already does this bring it to Instagram.

Key Takeaway

The Verdict

Zomato has built one of India's most recognisable brand voices on X witty, culturally savvy, and genuinely entertaining. But their Instagram strategy isn't keeping pace. The platform demands consistency, saves, and timing precision that their current approach isn't fully delivering.

The opportunity is massive. They have the audience, the brand equity, and the creative potential. They just need a more intentional approach to Instagram one that prioritises save-worthy value, visual cohesion, and trend agility over raw volume. More strategy, same boldness.

"Strategy is the difference between content and results."
