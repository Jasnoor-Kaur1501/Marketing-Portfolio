The Brief

The Opportunity

Nykaa is a powerhouse in women's beauty. But NykaaMan their men's grooming vertical is massively underutilised. The Indian men's grooming market is growing at 11% annually, Gen Z men are the most skincare-aware generation yet, and Nykaa already has the platform, the logistics, and the brand trust.

The problem? NykaaMan feels like an afterthought. The social content is sparse, the messaging is generic, and the brand voice doesn't resonate with its actual target audience. If I were CMO, here's how I'd fix that with a 4-week relaunch campaign.

Campaign Concept

The Big Idea

Campaign name: "Look Good. Don't Explain Yourself."

The insight: Men want to look good but the cultural script around men's grooming in India still carries a stigma the idea that caring about your appearance is somehow "not masculine." We flip that on its head without being preachy about it. No lecture. No campaign manifesto. Just men who look good and simply do not owe anyone an explanation for it.

The tone is confident, dry-humoured, and completely unselfconscious. Think: the brand knows, the guy knows, and we're all just getting on with it.

Pillar 1: Education

"Skincare basics for people who don't know where to start" destigmatise through usefulness, not preaching.

Pillar 2: Humour

Relatable, self-aware content. Men who've Googled "how to use a toner" at 1am. We see them.

⭐

Pillar 3: Social Proof

Real men, real reviews, real before-afters. Not aspirational credible.

️

Pillar 4: Product-Led

Show the product in use, not on a shelf. Tutorials, routines, "add to cart" moments.

Pillar 5: Culture

Trend-jack moments where grooming, fashion, and pop culture overlap. Stay in the conversation.

Pillar 6: Community

UGC, duets, challenges, reposts. Make the audience the content.

Rollout Plan

4-Week Timeline

Week 1
Tease & Intrigue
Zero product shown. Just the line "Look Good. Don't Explain Yourself." on black. Stories with countdown. Cryptic X posts. Build curiosity before the reveal get people asking what Nykaa is up to.
Week 2
Launch & Creator Blitz
Full campaign reveal. Simultaneously drop on Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and X. Activate 8 to 10 micro/mid-tier male creators (grooming, lifestyle, comedy niches) with gifted products and a creative brief. Each creator uses their own angle no scripts.
Week 3
Community & UGC Wave
Launch #LookGoodNoExplanation challenge. Encourage men to share their routines, their starter kits, their "I finally tried it" moments. Best UGC gets repotted and featured. Drive saves with "5 products every guy actually needs" carousels.
Week 4
Convert & Sustain
Retargeting ads for people who engaged. Push "starter kit" bundles with urgency. Post-campaign analytics review. Build the content blueprint for month 2 based on what performed.
Content Examples

What We'd Actually Post

Reel

"My 3-step routine. Yes, men have routines now."

60-second Reel POV of a guy doing his morning routine, zero narration, just the products and the result. Dry caption. High save potential.

Carousel

"5 NykaaMan products under ₹500 the starter kit"

Educational carousel targeting men who have no idea where to start. One product per slide, benefit-led copy, link in bio CTA. Designed to be saved and shared.

X

"Men who moisturize > [leave blank]" open-ended tweet

Engagement bait done right. Gets men defending themselves in the comments (which is engagement) and women agreeing (which is reach). Pin it for the week.

Story

Poll: "Do you have a skincare routine?" + product reveal link

Low-effort engagement on Stories. Segues perfectly into a product recommendation based on their answer. Personalisation at scale.
